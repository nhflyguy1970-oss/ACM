# Cognitive Activation Architecture — ACM

**Status:** Canonical shared architecture (M5)  
**First consumer:** Remembering  
**Future consumers:** Reflection · Learning · Prediction · Planning · Creativity · Analogical Reasoning · Goal Management · Attention refinement · Metacognition · assent-gated self-improvement  

**Companions:** [`SPREADING_ACTIVATION.md`](SPREADING_ACTIVATION.md) · [`REMEMBERING_MODEL.md`](REMEMBERING_MODEL.md) · [`ARCHITECTURE.md`](ARCHITECTURE.md)

## Purpose

Define **one** activation model for ACM. No future organ invents a parallel activation mechanism.

## What activation is

Transient excitation of cognitive objects (primarily Concepts; Associations as conduits; Experiences as evidence participants). Activation is cognitive state, not storage I/O.

## Why activation exists

Active cognition needs a common energy language so organs cooperate: Remembering reconstructs; later Reflection evaluates; later Learning adjusts enduring strengths — without reimplementing spread each time.

## Cue-driven activation

Cues enter as seeds. Cue classes (question / concept / goal / place / time / identity / working-residue) are **labels for observability**, not chain-of-thought.

## Propagation · thresholds · decay

Documented in [`SPREADING_ACTIVATION.md`](SPREADING_ACTIVATION.md). Architecture mandates: directional Association traversal, hop-bounded spread, decay, reconstruct/compete thresholds.

## Competing activations & inhibition

Multiple Concepts may activate in parallel. Lateral inhibition / relative ranking prevents every weak neighbor from joining reconstructions. Competitors near the top create ambiguity signals rather than silent winners.

## Working Memory interaction

Buffer occupants receive activation boosts. New reconstructed foci may enter the buffer (capacity interference remains Working Memory’s ownership).

## Context · Goal · Identity interaction

| Source | Effect on the field |
|--------|---------------------|
| Context tags | Boost matching Concept attributes / related Experiences’ tags |
| Active goals | Bias Concepts associated with goals |
| Identity | Bias identity Concepts; Who-queries seed from Identity organ |

## Concept activation · Association traversal · Experience participation

Concepts hold energy. Associations transmit it. Experiences are selected via Concept evidence/exemplars and co-activation — prefer Concepts→Associations over raw Experience scans (D014).

## Confidence propagation

Activation energy and enduring Concept/Association confidence are distinct. Reconstruction confidence combines both. Confidence deltas remain harness-observable.

## Salience & temporal influence

Salience overlays and recency of Experiences/Concepts bias seed and evidence selection without rewriting birth records.

## Parallel activation

Multiple seeds may fire in one remember episode. Wavefronts merge into one field with shared hop accounting.

## Future optimization

Hot-path caches, learned spreads, modality-specific cue adapters — always as consumers of **this** architecture. Changing thresholds/decay policy structurally requires future user authorization (self-improvement ≠ continuous Learning).

## Ownership rule

Activation Architecture owns the **field mechanics**. Organs own their questions:

| Organ | Owns |
|-------|------|
| Remembering | Reconstruction from the field |
| Identity | Who am I? |
| Experience | History |
| Concept | Meaning |
| Association | Relationships |
| Future Reflection | Evaluation of reconstructions |
